Transcribing the 1915 Iowa Census

Welcome!

Thank you for helping with transcribing data for the 1915 Iowa state census! This was the first census in the United States to record information on education and earnings, making it a uniquely helpful document for economic researchers to understand that era. (See here for more details.)

The current project is part of an ongoing effort by a group of researchers (see "about us" at the bottom) to understand Mobility in the United States during the depression era. To this end, we are interested in transcribing people's earnings, occupations and property values. The example below describes the fields we need transcribed:

An Example

Here's a card from the 1915 Iowa census, with the 9 fields we want to enter indicated. Of the 9 fields, only "Occupation" is a string. All others are (or should be!) numbers. For instance, Card no. is 1324, not B24! (Don't worry, we chose a difficult card as an example)

Earnings

Earnings is the annual earnings in 1914 (in $). This might be recorded with $ or ,'s or other things but we just need the numbers. If there is a word written here instead (like unemployed), write that instead. Fred Reynolds earned $800 in 1914.

Occupation

Occupation is a string that indicates what job the person had. In this case, Fred Reynolds was a Farmer. (Laborer and Housekeeper are two other common occupations in the data). This field might be hard to read, but do your best.

Education

The extent of education was asked in four categories: Common School, Grammar School, High School, and College. Not everyone will have years of all four categories, so just enter the years they report a number for and skip any with dashes or blanks. In this card, Fred Reynolds has 8 years of common school and 3 years of high school and nothing else. Some records may have a single number that spans both Common and Grammar lines. For these, enter the number in Common ONLY.

Farm/Home Incumbrance and Value

Like Earnings, these values should both be dollar amounts. In Fred's case, the Incumbrance on his farm or home is $2500, and the value of his farm/home is $10000. Usually, these values are only populated if the answer to a previous question ("Do you own your home or farm") is yes. But we don't you to fill in that question.

Handling Blanks or illegible text

If a field is empty on the card, or if there's a dash or a blank drawn through it (as shown), please leave it empty on the form. For numeric fields (such as income) only enter "0" if it is explicitly written on the card.

Enter "-1" if a field (of any type) is illegible or hard to read.
